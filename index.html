ABOUT MT. VERNON GARDEN

In the 1930’s an exact replica of one of Martha Washington’s gardens at Mount Vernon was constructed and still resides today in Wash Park.

The inspiration for the Wash Park garden came directly from one of the many gardens Martha Washington tended to when living at the astonishing Mount Vernon plantation. At the time the Washington’s resided at Mount Vernon the property spanned 8,000 acres and was divided into five farms. The Washington’s many gardens were filled with plants and flowers of all kinds and organized in many interesting ways. Some of the seeds planted were even sent all the way from friends in foreign countries. Today visitors are able to visit the vast property and still enjoy the gardens the former First Lady adored.

The modern day replica here in Denver holds very close the original garden. The attention to detail is really quite astonishing. The small stone retaining wall that surrounds the replica and allows visitors to step down into the gardens is representative of the original garden’s green house walls. The unique rectangle groupings of flowers throughout the garden were modeled after the original garden’s planters.

While the Martha Washington Garden is truly one of a kind, the surrounding gardens in the park are also a true site to be marveled at! It is estimated that over 25,000 flowers are planted in the park’s gardens annually with nearly 170,000 of those raised in the local City Park greenery. The design of the gardens is also completely redesigned every year to create the most optimal arrangements..
